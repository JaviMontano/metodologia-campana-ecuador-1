## 1) Intake Process

Every service proposal starts with structured intake. Never propose without this data:

### Mandatory Inputs

| Field | Description | Source |
|-------|------------|--------|
| **Client context** | Sector, industry, company size, maturity digital | Client brief or embajador |
| **Problem statement** | What pain or objective drives the request | Client in their words |
| **Target audience** | Who will participate? Roles, levels, quantity | Client brief |
| **Desired outcome** | What does "success" look like for the client? | Client |
| **Timeline constraint** | When do they need it? Fixed date or flexible? | Client |
| **Budget range** | If shared; otherwise, propose based on catalog pricing | Client or inferred |
| **Segment** | B2B, B2C, B2B2B co-brand, B2B2B white-label | Embajador assessment |
| **Prior services** | Has the client or participants done any MetodologIA service before? | CRM or embajador |

### Optional but Valuable Inputs

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| Current AI maturity | Informs starting point (niveles heterogeneos) |
| IT restrictions | AI tools allowed/blocked |
| Internal initiatives | Existing transformation programs |
| Decision-maker profile | Sponsor vs end-user dynamics |
| Competitive context | What alternatives are they considering |

## 2) Service DNA Constraints

Every proposed service — custom or new — MUST inherit these non-negotiable elements:

### Methodological DNA
1. **Five layers of amplification:** prompting → evaluation → reusable assets → automation → prototyping
2. **Practice-dominant pedagogy:** minimum 70% practice, maximum 15% demo, 15% verification
3. **Human-in-the-loop:** AI proposes, human validates and decides
4. **Verifiable outputs:** every module produces evidence; no module is "just talk"
5. **Contextualization:** exercises adapted to client sector/reality (included, not extra)

### Operational DNA
1. **Embajador + Facilitador separation** (except smallest operations)
2. **Brief → Preparation → Execution → Memories → Closure → Post-sale** cycle
3. **SLA documented:** Email D+1, WhatsApp <=2h (L-V 9-16h COT)
4. **Guarantee:** time-bounded refund with feedback requirement
5. **Credito educativo:** value transfers to next service in the ladder

### Brand DNA
1. **No absolute guarantees** — conditional language, realistic goals
2. **No marketing superlatives** in operational/contractual docs
3. **Zero jargon for inexperto** audiences
4. **Comparativa honesta** in commercial materials
5. **CTA always specific** — not "contactenos" but "agenda una llamada de 15 minutos"

## 3) Proposal Generation Method

### Step 1: Map against existing catalog

```
For each existing service in inventory:
  - Calculate OVERLAP SCORE: how many of the client's needs does this service cover?
  - Calculate GAP SCORE: what client needs are NOT covered?
  - Calculate EXCESS SCORE: what does this service include that the client doesn't need?
```

### Step 2: Decision matrix

| Scenario | Action |
|----------|--------|
| Overlap >= 80%, Gap <= 20% | **CONFIGURE:** Use existing service + minor customization |
| Overlap 50-79%, Gap 21-50% | **COMBINE:** Propose a bundle of 2 services or a configured version with additions |
| Overlap < 50%, Gap > 50% | **INNOVATE:** Design a new service from DNA constraints |

### Step 3: Generate proposal

For CONFIGURE:
- Identify base service
- List customizations needed (contextualization, additional modules, extended duration)
- Price = base price + customization surcharge (if any)
- Mark any scope extensions as [POR CONFIRMAR: pricing]

For COMBINE:
- Identify services to combine
- Design the bridge (how services connect, shared context, unified experience)
- Price = sum of services with bundling discount (recommend 10-15%)
- Clarify which credits apply

For INNOVATE:
- Design from DNA constraints (Section 2)
- Create canonical skeleton (Sec 0-15 per template)
- Price based on value ladder positioning
- Mark EVERYTHING as [POR CONFIRMAR] until JM validates

### Step 4: Safety review

Run every proposal through:
1. Legal safety checklist (`references/legal-safety-checklist.md`)
2. Brand voice rules (`references/brand-voice-rules.md`)
3. Self-care rules (SC-01 through SC-10)
4. Operational feasibility (can the team actually deliver this?)

## 4) Custom Configuration vs New Service

### When to CONFIGURE (preferred — lower risk)

- Client needs match 80%+ of an existing service
- Customization is limited to: contextualization, examples, exercises, minor time adjustments
- No new modules, no new methodology, no new certification
- Price stays within 15% of catalog price

### When to INNOVATE (higher risk — requires JM approval)

- No existing service covers >50% of needs
- Client represents a new vertical or use case
- Revenue opportunity justifies the design investment
- MetodologIA has the expertise to deliver (don't invent outside competence)

### Innovation guardrails

| Guardrail | Rationale |
|-----------|-----------|
| Max 3 new modules per proposal | More = untested → quality risk |
| New service must fit in value ladder | Entry/Profundizacion/Premium/Transformacion — no orphans |
| Pilot first | New designs execute as pilot before catalog inclusion |
| Pricing = existing tier +/- 20% max | Wild pricing creates catalog incoherence |
| JM approval required | No new service goes to catalog without explicit approval |
| [POR CONFIRMAR] on everything new | Until validated in execution |

## 5) Pricing Consistency Rules

### Value Ladder Pricing Bands

| Category | Duration range | B2C range (COP) | B2B range (COP) |
|----------|---------------|-----------------|-----------------|
| Entrada | 2-4h | 150.000-300.000 | 2.000.000-4.000.000 |
| Profundizacion | 15-25h | 600.000-1.200.000 | 8.000.000-15.000.000 |
| Premium | 15-25h (consultive) | 2.500.000-5.000.000 | 12.000.000-22.000.000 |
| Transformacion | 40-60h | 12.000.000-22.000.000 | 25.000.000-45.000.000 |

New services MUST price within +/- 20% of their category band. Outliers require explicit justification in [DECISION] tag.

### Custom configuration surcharges

| Type of customization | Surcharge | Justification |
|----------------------|-----------|---------------|
| Contextualización (sector, ejercicios, casos) | Incluido (COP 0) | Part of standard service |
| Additional module (2h) | COP 1.500.000-3.000.000 B2B | New content development |
| Extended session (per hour beyond standard) | COP 500.000-800.000 B2B | Facilitator time |
| Second facilitator | +50% of base price | Documented in canonicos |
| Rush delivery (<7 dias lead time) | +20-30% | Protects team capacity (Self-care rule SC-08) |

## 7) Risk Assessment

Every proposal gets a risk rating:

| Risk Level | Criteria | Action Required |
|------------|----------|----------------|
| **LOW** | Configuration of existing service, <20% customization | Embajador can present to client |
| **MEDIUM** | Combination of services or >20% customization | JM reviews before presenting |
| **HIGH** | New service design or new vertical | JM designs/approves; pilot execution mandatory |

Risk factors that increase level:
- New client in unknown sector (+1)
- B2B2B model (+1)
- Custom pricing outside band (+1)
- Timeline < 14 dias (+1)
- >30 participants (+1)
- Client requests outcomes MetodologIA hasn't validated (+1)
